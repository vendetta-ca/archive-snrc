Summary

A biological specimen of undetermined species has been observed intermittently in the Philippine Sea at approximately 800 metres depth. The specimen is large relative to known species in this stratum. Behaviour is consistent across observations. A working model of its movement patterns has been developed and is considered reliable. The specimen has not interacted with observation equipment.

Description

The specimen is estimated at 6–8 metres in length based on sonar return profiles. It has been visually confirmed by ROV on two occasions, though image resolution at operating depth has limited formal species identification. Coalition biologists have noted similarities to known deep-sea species in the family Somniosidae, though confirmation is pending further observation. Movement patterns are slow and consistent with known behaviour for large deep-sea specimens.

The specimen has been observed in the same geographic range across all monitoring sessions. Seasonal variation in depth has been noted and is consistent with known migration behaviour in comparable species.
