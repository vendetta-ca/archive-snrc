Summary

On 2031-04-09 at 04:51 UTC, hydrophone array ████-07 registered an anomalous low-frequency pulse originating at approximately 8,200 metres depth in the Tonga Trench. The pulse recurred at a fixed interval of 97.4 seconds for a total of ██ cycles before ceasing. At 05:18 UTC, telemetry from research vessel ████████, stationed at the surface directly above the event origin, was lost. No distress signal was received. Recovery operations have thus far failed to locate the vessel or its crew of six.

Acoustic profile

The pulse does not match any catalogued geological, cetacean, or anthropogenic source. Frequency analysis indicates a structured waveform with internal repetition suggesting — per the assessment of Dr. █████████, Pacific Division acoustics lead — possible intentionality. This assessment is disputed by ██████████████ and is pending peer review. Full acoustic data is available to Tier 3 clearance holders only.

Vessel loss

RV ████████ was a ██-metre research vessel operating under SNRC Pacific Division charter. Its last confirmed telemetry placed it at the surface, stationary, at the coordinates noted above. Post-event sonar sweeps of the area have returned ████████████████████████████████████████. The Coalition's official position is that the loss is under active investigation. Member nation ███████ has formally requested an independent inquiry; this request is under consideration by the Oversight Committee.

Current containment status

Entity SNRC-0091 has not been visually confirmed. Exclusion zone of 40 nautical miles has been established around the event coordinates under Coalition maritime authority. Two Tier 1 monitoring vessels are on station. Any vessel entering the exclusion zone without SNRC authorisation is to be intercepted and crew detained for debriefing.
