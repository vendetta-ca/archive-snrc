Summary

A low-frequency acoustic pulse was recorded by Pacific Division hydrophone array during routine monitoring operations in the Tonga Trench at approximately 8,200 metres depth. The pulse recurred at a fixed interval before ceasing. Origin has not been confirmed. Classification is Grade 3 due to disagreement between Pacific Division researchers regarding the pulse's source. Observation is ongoing. All personnel are accounted for.

Acoustic profile

The pulse recurred at a fixed interval of 97.4 seconds across all recorded cycles before ceasing. The waveform does not match catalogued geological, cetacean, or anthropogenic sources. Pacific Division researchers hold competing assessments: the majority position attributes the pulse to a geological source consistent with tectonic activity at this depth; a minority position notes internal waveform structure that the majority assessment does not fully account for. Both assessments are on record. The pulse has not recurred since the initial observation window.
