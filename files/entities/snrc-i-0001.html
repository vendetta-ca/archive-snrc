Summary

A cluster of bioluminescent organisms was observed during routine survey operations at 640 metres depth in the Java Sea. Light emission appears coordinated across the cluster. The phenomenon has been observed on four separate occasions over twelve years and has remained consistent in location, depth, and behaviour. No anomalous behaviour has been recorded.

Description

The cluster occupies an estimated area of 14 square metres and consists of an undetermined number of individual organisms. Light emission is blue-green in spectrum, consistent with known bioluminescent organisms in this stratum. Emission patterns are coordinated in the sense that the cluster pulses at a uniform rate rather than producing individual organism-level light events. This is consistent with documented colonial organism behaviour.

The cluster is stationary across all four observation windows. No movement relative to local current patterns has been recorded. ROV approaches up to 2 metres have produced no change in cluster behaviour.
