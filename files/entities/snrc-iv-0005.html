Summary

A structural formation consistent with a vessel hull was identified in the Atacama Trench at approximately 4,500 metres depth during a routine survey in 2009. The formation does not correspond to any vessel in the maritime registries of Coalition member nations. The structure is in good condition relative to its estimated age and depth. Classification is Grade 3 due to ongoing disagreement between Atlantic Division researchers regarding the formation's origin and the methodology of registry cross-referencing. All personnel are accounted for.

Physical description

The structure is approximately 60–70 metres in length and consistent with early-to-mid 20th century vessel construction. Hull integrity is noted as unusually good for the depth and estimated age. Registry searches across Coalition member nations have not produced a match. Research is ongoing. The structure has been externally surveyed on multiple occasions. Interior access has been attempted and is documented in the contact log.

Research notes

Interior surveys have produced inconsistent findings across observations. Atlantic Division researchers attribute inconsistencies to equipment limitations at depth and variable ROV camera positioning. A minority position within the division contests this assessment. Classification Review Board has noted the inconsistencies and assigned Grade 3 pending resolution. Further interior surveys are planned.
